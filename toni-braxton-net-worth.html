Toni Braxton’s Early Career and Breakthrough

Toni Braxton was born on October 7, 1967, in Severn, Maryland.

She grew up in a religious family and started singing in the church choir with her sisters.

She attended Bowie State University to pursue a career in teaching but dropped out after being discovered by a producer who heard her singing at a gas station.

In the late 1980s, Braxton formed a group with her sisters called The Braxtons.

They signed with Arista Records and released their first single “Good Life” in 1990.

The song was not a hit, and the group was dropped from the label.

However, Toni Braxton caught the attention of producers L.A. Reid and Babyface, who signed her to their newly formed label LaFace Records.

In 1993, Toni Braxton released her self-titled debut album, which was a huge success.

It reached number one on the Billboard 200 chart and sold 10 million copies worldwide.

It also spawned several hit singles, such as “Another Sad Love Song”, “Breathe Again”, and “You Mean the World to Me”.

The album earned Braxton three Grammy Awards, including Best New Artist.

Toni Braxton’s Income and Assets

Toni Braxton continued to dominate the charts with her second album Secrets in 1996.

The album sold over 15 million copies worldwide and featured two number one hits: “You’re Makin’ Me High” and “Un-Break My Heart”.

However, despite her massive success, Toni Braxton was not happy with her contract with LaFace Records.

She also filed for bankruptcy, citing debts of over $20 million.

Eventually, she settled with LaFace Records and signed a new deal with them.

In 2000, Toni Braxton released her third album The Heat, which debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 chart and sold over 4 million copies worldwide.
